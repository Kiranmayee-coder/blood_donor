About Blood Donation

Blood donation is a vital part of healthcare. Every two seconds, someone needs blood. A single blood donation can save up to three lives. Blood is essential for surgeries, cancer treatment, chronic illnesses, and traumatic injuries.

Only about 3% of age-eligible people donate blood yearly. Your donation can help ensure that supply meets demand. Blood donations are used for patients in need of surgery, cancer treatment, and transfusions for blood loss from traumatic injuries.

For cancer patients, blood transfusions can help replenish platelets after treatments like chemotherapy. Maintaining diversity in the blood supply is also crucial, as some blood types are rare and more likely to be found among people with shared ancestral origins.

Donating blood is a generous act that helps people in need and supports your community. There is no substitute for blood, and it is needed every day of the year.
